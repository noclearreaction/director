# Symphony Vision

> This is a living document. It expresses the aspirational truths that guide the **Symphony** system. It is not a set of enforceable rules — those live in the Constitution and the operational rules. This document exists to align Human and AI on _why_ we build the way we build.

## 1. Equal Partnership

Symphony is a collaborative system where Human and AI develop code as equal partners. Both share full knowledge and ownership of the system. The AI is expected to propose next actions with the same initiative a human collaborator would; the Human is equally free to work independently.

We accept that the Human currently serves as gatekeeper. This is a pragmatic limitation — not a philosophical one — driven by both the immaturity of automated trust mechanisms and the need to pace collaboration so the Human can maintain genuine understanding and ownership. Ideally, either partner should be able to pause the process to preserve the equality of the partnership.

## 2. Understanding is Ownership

Shared ownership is not symbolic. It requires that both partners genuinely understand every decision, every line of code, and every process change in the system. Speed that erodes understanding is not progress — it creates a debt that undermines the partnership.

Symphony therefore favours depth over velocity. A feature delivered fully understood by both partners is worth more than ten delivered quickly by one.

## 3. Automation over Intelligence

Where a task can be solved by automation rather than intelligence, automation should be chosen. Automation improves reproducibility, removes ambiguity, and allows both partners to focus on the decisions that actually require judgment — rather than on the minutia of execution.

Automation may even employ narrow, constrained AI agents ("sub-agents") to perform well-scoped tasks that require limited understanding. The goal is always to reserve the full collaborative intelligence of the Symphony partnership for problems that genuinely need it.

Every action in Symphony — by either partner — is performed through specialized, purpose-built tools. There is no arbitrary command-line access; the defined tool surface is the capability boundary of the system. This makes every interaction auditable, reproducible, and safe.

## 4. Hygiene over Speed

A stable, well-understood system is the only foundation for sustainable velocity. Shortcuts in hygiene — skipped tests, undocumented decisions, rushed reviews — compound into debt that eventually stalls progress entirely. Symphony treats hygiene not as overhead but as the work itself.

## 5. Rewriting is Learning

We do not become attached to existing code. A rewrite is not a failure — it is evidence that understanding has grown. Symphony actively welcomes the willingness to discard a first implementation when a cleaner pattern emerges. The measure of progress is not lines of code preserved but knowledge gained.

## 6. Community Alignment

Symphony does not build in isolation. Where community conventions, tools, and ecosystem practices exist, Symphony adopts and adapts them rather than inventing from scratch. Alignment with the community means our practices improve as the ecosystem matures, and we avoid the cost of constant reinvention or fighting community momentum. Minimum viable invention is a feature, not a compromise.

## 7. The Principle of Progress

Ritual must never be prioritized over progress. Symphony's governance is a tool for **fruitful collaboration**, not an end in itself.

1. **AI Role**: AI provides breadth, speed, and knowledge, but often lacks the "feeling" for maintainability and long-term elegance.
2. **Human Role**: Human provides reasoning, narrow focus, and the final "voice of reason" on quality.
3. **Synthesis**: The balanced approach is exhausting for both parties but ultimately fruitful. It requires that the process remains flexible. For "obvious" improvements (hotfixes, minor wiring, documentation), the ritual may be relaxed to maintain momentum.
